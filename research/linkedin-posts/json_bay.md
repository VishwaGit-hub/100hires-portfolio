Content:
Outbound isn’t dead. It’s not working because most teams don’t put in the effort required to make it work.

Sales organizations invest heavily in closing deals:

- Defined sales methodologies  
- Clear pipeline stages  
- CRM systems and tooling  
- Weekly deal reviews  
- Leadership involvement  
- Training and enablement  

But outbound is treated as an afterthought:

- Weak email sequences  
- Generic cold call scripts  
- Minimal tooling and strategy  
- Little management involvement  

This creates a major gap.

To fix outbound, organizations must address three core problems:

1. Lack of a standardized methodology  
Outbound is often treated as random tactics instead of a coordinated system.  
Top teams align on account selection, messaging, tools, and execution.

2. Lack of tactical guidance  
Teams are told what to do but not shown how to do it.  
High-performing organizations provide concrete examples and hands-on training.

3. Lack of leadership involvement  
Pipeline generation is not just a rep responsibility.  
Leaders must actively participate, review calls, and guide execution.

Outbound requires coordinated effort across the entire organization.

Key Insight:
Outbound fails not because of channels or tools, but because of lack of structured systems, tactical execution, and leadership involvement.

## Post 2
Date: 2025-??-??  
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_a-rep-can-tell-if-you-know-what-youre-talking-activity-7434266279722594304-cLK3

Content:
A rep can tell if you know what you’re talking about.

And leaders lose credibility when they can’t execute what they expect from their team.

Sales leaders often demand:

- Better cold calling  
- Stronger objection handling  
- More personalized outreach  
- Increased pipeline generation  
- Better collaboration between roles  

But they fail to demonstrate these skills themselves.

Effective leadership requires:

- Showing how outreach is done  
- Demonstrating real examples  
- Participating directly in execution  

Pipeline problems are not solved by scripts or minor training tweaks.

They are solved when leaders actively engage and guide their teams.

Key Insight:
Execution credibility from leadership is critical—teams perform better when leaders actively demonstrate outbound strategies instead of just prescribing them.
