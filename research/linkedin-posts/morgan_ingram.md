I worked with an SDR team that was sending one LinkedIn message per prospect and getting no responses.

The fix was simple:

Follow up.

Most teams send one message, get no reply, and move on.

This treats LinkedIn like a slot machine—hoping one message will “hit.”

Instead, use a structured follow-up system:

Attempt 1: Lead with something specific about the prospect  
Attempt 2: Change format (voice note, video, or different angle)  
Attempt 3: Use a lighter touch (e.g., GIF or informal message)  

Each follow-up should introduce a new angle, not repeat the same message.

In a workshop, applying this approach generated 10 responses within a week, including booked meetings.

Mixing LinkedIn with email and phone increases effectiveness further.

Persistence—done correctly—is critical for outbound success.

Key Insight:
Consistent, multi-angle follow-ups significantly outperform single-touch outreach and are essential for generating pipeline.

Content:
An effective outbound strategy can start with analyzing a company’s careers page.

Hiring patterns reveal business priorities and potential pain points.

Workflow:

1. Identify job titles that indicate a need for your solution  
Example: hiring SDRs or AEs with pipeline responsibilities signals a need for outbound support  

2. Use AI tools to analyze job descriptions  
Extract:
- Pain points  
- Decision-makers  
- Key observations relevant to outreach  

3. Use these insights in outbound messaging  
For example:
- Share a screenshot of the job posting  
- Reference specific hiring signals  
- Connect them directly to your solution  

This approach creates highly relevant outreach that stands out.

The advantage is not just using AI—but connecting insights meaningfully.

Key Insight:
Effective outbound relies on identifying real business signals (like hiring activity) and using them to create highly relevant, insight-driven outreach.
