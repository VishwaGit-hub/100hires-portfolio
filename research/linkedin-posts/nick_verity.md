Follow-up is a craft.

One of our reps significantly increased close rates by building a structured follow-up system.

She customized her CRM workflow to match how she manages leads daily and executes with a consistent rhythm.

Her approach:

Before sales calls:
She sends highly personalized emails, sometimes longer and detailed.

Between calls:
She directly calls warm leads.

At the right moment:
She sends short, personal text messages.

The key is consistency and human interaction across touchpoints.

This creates:

- Faster responses  
- Smoother deal progression  
- Continuity in conversations  

This structured follow-up cadence helped her move from a low performer to a top performer.

Key Insight:
A structured, multi-channel follow-up system tailored to individual workflow significantly improves conversion and deal velocity.

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-verity_a-dangerous-assumption-a-lot-of-b2b-teams-activity-7450586117566857217-JdB9

Content:
Many B2B teams assume that posting on LinkedIn is enough.

But posting alone does not generate pipeline.

Content becomes valuable only when it:

- Clarifies positioning so buyers understand what you do  
- Builds trust before outreach  
- Supports outbound messaging  

Most content fails because it focuses on visibility instead of conversion.

It generates impressions without intent.

Companies that generate pipeline from LinkedIn do not just post more—they connect content to their outbound strategy.

Content must align with:

- Target audience  
- Messaging  
- Sales motion  

Otherwise, it becomes noise.

The key question is not consistency, but whether content makes outbound more effective.

Key Insight:
Content is only valuable when integrated with outbound strategy—otherwise it drives visibility without pipeline impact.
