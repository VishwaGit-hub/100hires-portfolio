Content:
If you feel nervous making cold calls, don’t try to eliminate the nerves.

Trying to suppress the feeling often makes it stronger.

Instead, observe it.

Shift from:
“I’m nervous”

To:
“Nerves are showing up”

This creates distance between you and the emotion.

When you create that separation, you can still act:

Call anyway  
Speak anyway  
Ask the question anyway  

Nervousness is not a blocker—it’s a normal part of the process.

The goal is not to remove discomfort, but to operate despite it.

Key Insight:
Emotional regulation in outbound is about detachment, not elimination—top performers act despite discomfort instead of waiting for confidence.

## Post 2
Date: 2025-??-??  
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_buyers-dont-choose-the-best-solution-they-activity-7455594474698657794-grwC

Content:
Buyers do not choose the best solution—they choose the safest one.

A prospect can agree your product is a perfect fit and still not move forward.

The reason is perceived risk.

Choosing a new solution introduces fears:

- If it fails, responsibility falls on the buyer  
- If implementation goes poorly, they are accountable  
- If productivity drops, they are blamed  
- If the team resists, it reflects on them  

This is why companies often stick with outdated systems or well-known vendors.

Not because they are better—but because they feel safer.

This aligns with the concept of “anxiety of the new”:

- Fear of breaking something  
- Fear of negative perception  
- Fear of career impact  

To close deals, it is not enough to prove value.

You must reduce perceived risk:

- Show successful examples from similar customers  
- Make onboarding simple and clear  
- Break adoption into low-risk steps  
- Offer ways to test before full commitment  
- Address unspoken concerns directly  

When buyers feel safe, they move forward.  
When they feel exposed, they delay or reject.

Key Insight:
Reducing perceived risk is more critical than demonstrating value—buyers prioritize safety over optimization in decision-making.
