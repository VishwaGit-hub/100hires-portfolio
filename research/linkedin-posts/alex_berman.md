Content:
When I get a cold email, my first thought is always the same:

Is this spam?

And that might sound surprising coming from me.

I’m Alex Berman - author of Cold Email Manifesto and the guy who’s helped 10,000+ businesses close deals with cold outreach.

If anyone should give cold emails the benefit of the doubt, it’s me.

And today…

This cold email almost had me.

It got delivered. The subject line was clean. The first few lines pulled me in.

But then… it lost me.

Here’s where it went wrong:

Subject: We did the math Alex…

Hey Alex - found your account on X and decided to write this out…

We ran the numbers…

Right now Alexberman is leaving $10,000+ of rev on the table each month by not using our Google strategy.

(Not to mention your competition is literally stealing your traffic as I write this.)

Cool if I sent a Loom showing how we’d solve this and get that revenue back?

BTW - we've done it for SaaS time and time again, happy to share some case studies.

So… why didn’t this work?

Avoid these 3 mistakes:

1. The offer is a mess  
The email starts with X but suddenly switches to Google strategy.  
The “ran the numbers” claim feels unverified and random.  
If the offer doesn’t make sense, the prospect disengages immediately.

2. The CTA is unclear  
The email asks for permission to send a Loom but also mentions case studies.  
It’s not clear what the recipient will receive.  
Unclear CTAs create hesitation and reduce replies.

3. The revenue claim makes no sense  
A flat $10K/month claim isn’t meaningful across different business sizes.  
For large companies, it’s insignificant.  
For smaller ones, it may feel unrealistic.

Rewritten version:

Subject: Quick question

Hey Alex - just checked your Google rankings and noticed you’re not showing up for [specific keyword].

Right now, [competitor’s name] is ranking #1 and pulling in the traffic that should be yours.

I’ve helped over 500 agency owners rank first for competitive keywords and I broke down why this is happening + how to fix it in a 2-min Loom.

Want to see it?

Key Insight:
Cold emails fail primarily due to unclear positioning, mismatched offers, and vague CTAs—not delivery or formatting.

Content:
I built an AI that writes perfect cold emails.

Most AI cold emails fail because they lack the right offer, targeting, and case studies.

There are two types of people:

Action takers — who send GPT-generated emails quickly  
Overthinkers — who refine offer, target, and messaging before sending

In most situations, speed works.

But in cold email, poor-quality emails lead to failed campaigns.

This results in what many call:
"cold email doesn't work"

To fix this, I built a system using n8n:

- Scrapes company data using Serper  
- Uses ChatGPT with a structured training dataset  
- Identifies and validates the correct offer  
- Defines the target market precisely  
- Suggests LinkedIn Sales Navigator targeting  
- Finds and adapts relevant case studies  
- Generates up to 3 high-quality emails using proven templates  

If the output isn’t strong, it is discarded.

Key Insight:
Cold outreach performance depends more on validated targeting and offer clarity than on the email copy itself.
