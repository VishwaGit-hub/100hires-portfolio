Content:
Your prospect Googled you before the call. The least you could do is return the favor.

Yet many reps still open with basic questions like:

"So, tell me about your company…"  
"What do you do?"  
"Who are your competitors?"  

These are questions that could be answered with minimal preparation.

Every unnecessary question signals to the buyer that you didn’t prepare.

Strong preparation builds trust and differentiates you as an advisor, not just a seller.

Here are five ways to improve pre-call research:

1. Understand company priorities  
Review earnings calls, reports, press releases, and leadership interviews to understand what the business is focused on right now.

2. Analyze job postings  
Hiring patterns reveal where the company is investing and what problems they are trying to solve.

3. Study your contact’s digital footprint  
Review their LinkedIn activity, content, and public discussions to align your messaging with their interests.

4. Map the buying committee  
Identify stakeholders before the call using LinkedIn and org charts.

5. Understand competitors  
Know the competitive landscape and identify opportunities to position your solution effectively.

Preparation signals respect and increases credibility.

Key Insight:
Effective outreach extends beyond messaging—pre-call research significantly impacts trust, positioning, and conversion.

Content:
You’re on a cold call with a decision-maker, you’ve identified real pain, but they won’t commit to a next step.

Common responses include:

“Call back in 6 months”  
“Send me an overview”  
“Shoot me an email”  

Instead of pushing harder, try this approach:

“I’ll follow up with an email summarizing our conversation. It’ll be in your inbox shortly.

Would you be open to introducing me to the person on your team who runs campaigns? I’d like to understand your process better and provide a more detailed solution.”

If possible, reference the person by name.

The goal is to maintain momentum by expanding engagement within the account.

Even if the initial contact stalls, a warm introduction creates new opportunities.

Key Insight:
When direct progression stalls, redirecting outreach to additional stakeholders helps maintain momentum and expand opportunities within accounts.
